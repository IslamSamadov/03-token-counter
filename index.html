What is an LLM?

A Large Language Model (LLM) is a type of artificial intelligence trained on vast amounts of text data. It learns patterns in language and can generate human-like text, answer questions, write code, summarise documents, and much more.

LLMs power tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. They work by predicting the most likely next token in a sequence, one step at a time, until a complete response is formed.

What is a Token?

A token is the basic unit of text that an LLM processes. It can be a whole word, part of a word, or even a single character. On average, one token equals roughly 3–4 characters of English text.

When you send a prompt to an LLM, your text is first broken into tokens. Both the input and output are counted in tokens — and pricing is typically based on how many tokens you use.
